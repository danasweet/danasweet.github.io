SomebodyStopMe

I came up with the idea for SomebodyStopMe in hopes of making public transit less painful. When I first moved to Chicago and started riding the bus, the only way I could tell when my stop was approaching was to stare at my phone and watch my location change on Google Maps. Even now, as a seasoned bus-rider, I often get lost in a daydream or a text conversation and manage to nearly miss my stop. I envisioned an app that I could turn on when I got on the bus, and it would alert me when I was getting close to my stop.

SomebodyStopMe takes your final destination address and the bus route that you're on and is able to determine the closest bus stop to where you're going. Once you get within a certain radius of that stop, the app sends a notification pop-up, as well as a sound and vibration. The app can run in the background, or you can watch the trip live--similar to Google Maps. If you still manage to miss your stop, SomebodyStopMe will send you another notification letting you know that if you're still on the bus, you've gone too far.

As a bonus feature, we added the ability to save favorite destinations, so if you take the same trip every day, you don't have to plug in the address each time. Favorites are easily located from the homepage, saved from the map page, and deleted from the favorites page.

SomebodyStopMe was a group effort for a DBC final project, and I served as team lead. The app was built in 8 days using Swift 2.0 and runs on Rails, backed by a PostgreSQL database with the postGIS extension.
